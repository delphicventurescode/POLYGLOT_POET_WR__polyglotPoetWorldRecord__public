## 🌌 Project Overview

This world record represents a unique intersection of prolific literary production, deep linguistic exploration, and creative expression. By bridging the worlds of natural languages and constructed languages (conlangs), this project pushes the boundaries of contemporary poetry and challenges traditional notions of literary mediums.

### The Record at a Glance
* **Core Achievement:** Most poetry books published within a two-year consecutive window.
* **Linguistic Diversity:** Spans both widely spoken **natural languages** and logically structured **constructed languages**.
* **Core Significance:** Demonstrates that constructed languages are not merely tools for utility or world-building, but are rich, legitimate mediums for high literary art, emotional depth, and poetic nuance.

## 🎯 Requirements & Scope

To satisfy the parameters of this specific record, the following criteria must be strictly met and verified:
1. **Temporal Constraint:** All qualifying volumes must be published within a strict window of two consecutive years.
2. **Linguistic Cross-Over:** The complete body of work must include at least one poetry book written in a constructed language and at least one poetry book written in a natural language.
3. **Poetic Integrity:** Each volume must stand as a distinct literary work, showcasing authentic poetic form, structural constraints, or thematic development.
